Strategy D

Buy the dip

Find a quality name suffering a temporary news-driven or earnings-reaction drop. Use AI to judge bounce-back probability, then validate financials/statistics, time with RSI, and enter near the bottom.

The essence: a temporary dip, not a trend break. The AI bounce-back verdict is a soft gate — a "falling-knife" verdict strongly discourages entry even if other steps pass.

Market Data — Live Prices & Fundamentals
Provider cheat-sheet. Finnhub: real-time US stocks + indices ETFs (SPY/QQQ/DIA/GLD), solid free tier, CORS-OK. Twelve Data: stocks/forex/indices/crypto (XAU/USD, ^GSPC, BTC/USD, USD/NGN), CORS-OK. Alpha Vantage: quotes + rich fundamentals (PE, ROE, margins, revenue growth), CORS-OK, but 25 requests/day on free tier — use it sparingly for fundamentals only. Polygon.io: excellent fundamentals + news, CORS-OK, free tier is end-of-day. Yahoo Finance: no direct browser access — you need a lightweight proxy (see deployment guide). NGX stocks aren't reliably available on any free API.
Fundamentals are fetched on demand (per-ticker), not on the refresh timer — financial statements update quarterly, so there's no benefit to polling them.

AI Integrations — 10 Slots
Browser CORS note: Most AI providers will block direct browser requests. Providers known to allow it: Anthropic (with header anthropic-dangerous-direct-browser-access: true), Google Gemini REST, some OpenAI-compatible endpoints (OpenRouter, Groq, Together). For OpenAI direct, you will likely need a CORS proxy or a lightweight relay server. API keys are stored only in your browser's localStorage — never exported unless you choose "Download Backup".
News Feed — for Market Intelligence
Auto-refresh runs every hour while the portfolio page is open and the checkbox is enabled. Manual news can always be added on the portfolio page regardless of provider config.
Data
